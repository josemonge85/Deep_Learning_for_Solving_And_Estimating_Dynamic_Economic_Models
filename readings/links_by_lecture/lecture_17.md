# Readings, Lecture 17: Deep uncertainty quantification and Pareto-improving climate policy

Default policy: link only.

## Deep uncertainty in IAMs

- **Folini, Friedl, Kübler, Scheidegger (2025), "The climate in climate economics,"** *Review of Economic Studies*, 92(1). [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1093/restud/rdae011). Identifies the parameters where the deep uncertainty actually bites; the CDICE model behind this lecture's notebook.
- **Friedl, Kübler, Scheidegger, Usui (2023), "Deep uncertainty quantification: with an application to integrated assessment models,"** working paper. The pseudo-state + GP + Sobol/Shapley methodology this lecture's notebook follows.
- **Fernández-Villaverde, Gillingham, Scheidegger (2025), "Climate change through the lens of macroeconomic modeling,"** *Annual Review of Economics*, 17. Survey context.

## Pareto-improving carbon taxation

- **Kübler, Scheidegger, Surbek (2026, forthcoming), "Using machine learning to compute constrained optimal carbon tax rules,"** *Journal of Political Economy: Macroeconomics*; arXiv:2507.01704. The OLG-IAM Pareto-improving-tax pipeline of §11.5; the production code is in the companion repository [`sischei/JPE_Macro_Using_ML_to_compute_constrained_optimal_carbon_tax_rules`](https://github.com/sischei/JPE_Macro_Using_ML_to_compute_constrained_optimal_carbon_tax_rules).
- **Krueger, Kübler (2006), "Pareto-improving social security reform when financial markets are incomplete!?,"** *American Economic Review*, 96(3). The Pareto-improvement criterion the carbon-tax design borrows.
- **Karp, Peri, Rezai (2024), intergenerational climate policy.** Recent work on the intergenerational-fairness angle.
- **Kotlikoff, Kübler, Polbin, Sachs, Scheidegger, "Making carbon taxation a generational win-win,"** *International Economic Review*; and **Kotlikoff, Kübler, Polbin, Scheidegger, "Pareto-improving carbon-risk taxation,"** working paper. Background on generationally-fair carbon taxation.

## Sensitivity analysis

- **Sobol (2001), "Global sensitivity indices for nonlinear mathematical models and their Monte Carlo estimates,"** *Mathematics and Computers in Simulation*, 55(1). The Sobol-index reference.
- **Owen (2014), "Sobol' indices and Shapley value,"** *SIAM/ASA J. Uncertainty Quantification*, 2(1). The Shapley-effects companion.
